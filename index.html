The Libraries

When we write code, it is possible to import code from other files, usually because it's a file full of useful functions that we want to use multiple times but only write once, we call these libraries. You can import a whole library or by uses braces (These things ->{ }) you can pick which functions you want to pull out of the library. Naturally the decoded library name that we import from is a clue as to the contextual use of the functions listed.

above & so

The Library name Trismegistus points us to Hermes Trigsmestus the father of Hermeticism and what would eventually evolve into Alchemy. one of his most prolific works is the Emerald Tablet, which besides declaring his rules of the universe is the origin of the phrase "As Above So Below". 

void* below = x => compose(so, () => above)(x);

The compose() function in a lot of languages takes multiple functions glues them all toegether and returns them as a single function that does all the functions in order. So this line is creating a function called below that takes an argument called x then apparently making it the same as whatever above is. So surprise surprise. "As above, so below". Or for the purposes of ultrakill "That which is in heaven, is also in hell"This will be important later.

permutation, transmutation & exhalation

The Library name principle along with one of the functions being transmutation points us towards the processes of alchemy, specifically the 3 principles of alchemy are Salt, Sulfur and Mercury. Alchemy changed a lot over the centuries and the theories evolved but by the 16th century it was put forward by an alchemist called Paracelsus that all matter in the world consisted of these 3 principles, the tria prima, in different ratios. The principles have innate properties that will manifest in matter based on the ratio of principles in said matter.

  • Sulfur is hot and dry, It is evaporation and expansion. It's symbol is 🜍
  • Mercury is cold and wet. It is fusibility and volatility. It's symbol is ☿
  • Salt is hard and inert. It is stability and structure. It's symbol is 🜔

This manifests in games with the new look skull keys. The skulls come in red, blue and now white and now carry alchemical symbols. This may be a connection to the menu pseudocode but this hasn't been confirmed and regardless is at least in part to make the game more accessible for colorblind people.

The red skull is sulfur. It's altar is a triangle and when you put a red skull on it, it heats up hot coals and begins to sizzle.

The blue skull is mercury. It's altar is a is round and when you put a blue skull on it, it fills with fluid.

The white skull is salt. It's altar is a is hexagonal with a line through it. There have been no gettable instances of the skull in the game but since the update it has been added all over the place as parts of level decoration and there are files in the games assets that support them working as the other keys.

So why are we pulling functions from a library named princple if these aren't the princples. Because so far we have just been importing functions. So they aren't the principles, they are things we can do to the principles.

  • Transmutation is the alchemical term for one matter being turned into another matter through manipulation, burning, boiling, washing with acids etc. This one is easy. Transmutation and alchemy go hand in hand.
    This is the function for changing a substance.
  • Exhalation is a little more obscure. As said before, Alchemy and it's terms and theories evolved over thousands of years. Parcelsus's tria prima idea was built on the work of centuries. Long before, a greek philosopher called Aristotle published writings on his explanations for why the world was the way it was. The writings spanned multiple books, collectively called Meteorlogica. He covered all kinds of things but for our purposes we are interested in book 3.

    We maintain that there are two exhalations, one vaporous the other smoky, and there correspond two kinds of bodies that originate in the earth, 'fossiles' and metals. The heat of the dry exhalation is the cause of all 'fossiles'[...] The vaporous exhalation is the cause of all metals [...] Their dryness compresses it, and it congeals just as dew or hoar-frost does when it has been separated off, though in the present case the metals are generated before that segregation occurs.

    ~ Aristotle

    What?

    You may notice here 3 interesting terms used. Exhalation, vapourous and smoky. Simply put, Aristotle refers to exhalations multiples times whenever he talks about the movement of air. He uses it for wind, but also the movement generated by heat rising. Alchemists over the years to come would interpret his writings as claiming that all metals and minerals in the earth are caused by exhalations from the earth the exhalations are either sulfurous smokes or mercurial vapours and their combinations formed everything they found in the ground from gold to granite.

    This is the function for acquiring a substance.
  • Permutation is not strictly an alchemical action. Permutation is a mathematical term that takes a set of items (say for example the numbers 1, 2, 3 and 4) and then figures out how many ways that set can be organised in a unique order. Permutation is where the alchemical touches the digital, if this code is to perform some form of alchemy it needs to be automated, permutations gives the code multiple combinations of matter to mess with.

    • This is the function for itterating a substance.

blood & formal_blood

Blood plays a large part in ultrakill's world, it is the source of all life, even if you can move, speak and think, if you do not have blood, you are not alive. Not everything that has blood is alive, but everything that is alive has blood. It appears that the womb here is literal. A container from which life can spring, maybe it's some horrific machine or a weird relic, cool to think about. But why are there two things? Why is blood different than formal_blood? To explore this we will begin to look at some of the other functions that we skipped earlier. 

void* reify = partial_apply(below, transmutation);
void* deify = #MACRO_INVERT(x) partial_apply(reify, x);

typedef matter(t_form) = #DERIVE reify(t_form);

These functions can be initially confusing, and can if misinterpreted lead you down the wrong path. Deify does not technically reference the divine, formal_blood is not formal in the way a 3 piece suit is formal. All this code refers to the work of Plato and his Theory of Forms

Again, what?

Plato's theory of forms was one of the early attempts to broker concepts of what reality even is. The theory of forms is big and complicated, but for the sake of this mystery we only need to know 2 things. Two of the main forms that anything can be is the ideal form and the platonic form. Put your thinking caps on.

An ideal form is something that isn't physical, you cannot hold it, create it, destroy it etc. but you know what it is. Some examples of ideal forms are beauty, mathematics, wisdom, violence. You can look at multiple beautiful objects and each object may be utterly different from the last but they all possess the form of beauty.

A platonic form is easier. A platonic form could be an object. It could be physical, it could be created it could be destroyed. A hat is a platonic form, so is a planet and a gun. No platonic forms exist because our universe is too chaotic for anything to be created at that level of purity, but what matters is that it technically could, but it never will.

With this context when we look at blood and formal_blood this now makes more sense. Blood is blood, the red goopy liquid we all love and keep in our bodies for fun and profit. Formal blood is the perfect form of blood beyond the physical. It is blood outside of limitations of space and time it is the most blood that blood can be.

But forms don't do anything, they just exist, so now we need to do something with them. This is where reify and deify come in.

  • reify takes something, mixes it with substances from within hell, commonly referred to as "hell matter" in game lore and applies alchemical transmutation to them to make them real within the physical world. The world reify is actually used in the theory of forms as something you cannot do. To reify something is to take the abstract, perfect form of a thing and treat it like it's a real physical object which as we've established you cannot do to forms. They cannot exist in our universe. Yet here we are reifying forms of things through alchemy. This is confirmed by the typedef (type definition. By defining a type you are saying "this is a type of thing, all things of the same type will follow the same rules and behave the same way.") line. It defines matter. Matter as in everything our physical reality is made out of every atom and molecule of our universe. #DERIVE usually means to basically do whatever you're deriving to the type you're defining. So if you're going to do stuff to matter with this code, it must be made real through transmutation.
  • deify does not refer to deity, or at least not literally. The function in simple terms does the opposite of reify. But whereas reify only applies to below, ergo all that is in hell. Deify applies to all that is in heaven AND hell (scroll up to the Trismegistus bit about as above so below) and casts it as an ideal form outside of reality.
